Before heading to the Barangay Hall to process your document (such as a Barangay Clearance, Certificate of Residency, Indigency, Cedula, or Blotter), please make sure you are fully prepared to ensure a smooth transaction.

 

What to Bring:

 

  • Valid Government-issued ID: Bring at least one original and a photocopy (e.g., PhilID, Driver’s License, Passport, UMID, or Student ID).
  • Proof of Residency: Any recent utility bill (electricity, water, or internet) under your name or your landlord's name to prove you live in the barangay.
  • Previous/Expired Documents: If you are renewing a clearance or a Cedula from last year, bringing the old copy can speed up the look-up process.
  • Authorization Letter: If you are processing a document on behalf of a family member, bring a signed authorization letter along with their valid ID.

What to Do to Be Prepared:

 

  • Know Your Specific Purpose: Be ready to state exactly why you need the document (e.g., Job Application, Local Employment, School Enrollment, Bank Requirements), as this is printed directly onto the clearance.
  • Prepare Cash for Fees: While some certificates (like Indigency) are often free for qualified residents, others carry processing fees. Please prepare ₱50 to ₱150 to cover document fees, community tax certificates (Cedula), or documentary stamps.
  • Dress Appropriately: Most Barangay Halls enforce a basic dress code for entry. Avoid wearing tank tops, sando, short shorts, or slippers.
  • Bring Your Own Pen: Save time and maintain hygiene by packing your own black or blue ballpoint pen for filling out application forms.
